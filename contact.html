Before You Ask

Questions we
always hear.

How much does a typical project cost?
It depends entirely on scope, but as a guide: brand identity projects start at £18,000, website projects start at £22,000, and full brand-plus-digital engagements typically run £45,000–£90,000. We don't cut scope to hit a number — we'd rather be honest about fit from the start.
How long does a project take?
Brand identity projects take 10–16 weeks. Website projects run 12–18 weeks. Combined engagements are 18–26 weeks. We build realistic timelines because we've seen the damage of rushed ones. Rush fees exist, but we'd prefer not to use them.
Do you work with early-stage startups?
Yes — some of our best work has been with founders at Series A and earlier. What matters is the ambition of the idea, not the current size of the company. Budget is a real constraint; if yours is early, let's talk about what's possible at your stage.
Do you work with clients outside the UK?
Entirely. About 60% of our clients are based outside the UK — across Europe, North America, the Middle East, and Africa. Our Cape Town studio makes us well-placed for African and time-zone-adjacent work. Remote collaboration is standard practice for us.
Who will actually work on our project?
Senior team members on every project, always. We don't take a brief and hand it to juniors. The people you meet in the pitch are the people who build your project. Our core team stays at eight people specifically to maintain that standard.
What do you need from us to get started?
An honest conversation is enough to begin. You don't need a polished brief, a complete budget, or a final decision. Tell us what you're building and why it matters. We'll ask the right questions from there — it's what we do.
